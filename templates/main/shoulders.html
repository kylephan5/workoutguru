Overhead Barbell Shoulder Press

The overhead shoulder press with a barbell is the quintessential compound shoulder exercise. It engages the front deltoids primarily, but also hits the lateral deltoids and the triceps. At the peak of the movement, make sure that your ears are right next to your biceps. Minimize momentum by not bouncing/using your legs.

- - -

Typically, you want to start your workout with the bench press, as it is a pretty taxing exercise. If you are looking to put on more mass, opt for heavier weight with less reps. However, if you are using this as a supplementary shoulder exercise, aim for higher repetition amounts.

Intended Goal Amount of Reps
Muscle Growth 6-12
Strength Training 3-5

Arnold Press

The Arnold press is a popular shoulder exercise done by Arnold Schwarzenegger. It targets the front deltoids from a seated bench position with some wrist supination.

- - -

For this exercise, you want to focus on hypertrophy and working the muscles through volume. We recommend that you use moderate weight where you are able to do 8-12 repetitions, with the last few repetitions being particularly difficult. Do not sacrifice form for weight, as you could increase risk for injury.

Intended Goal Amount of Reps
Muscle Growth 8-12
Strength Training 3-5

Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press

The seated dumbbell shoulder press is a great exercise that targets the front deltoid primarily. Be sure to keep tension on the front deltoid by not bringing your arms too far down in between reps. In addition, focus on mind muscle connection throughout the exercise.

- - -

For this exercise, you want to focus on hypertrophy and working the muscles through volume. We recommend using a moderately heavy weight where you are able to perform 8-12 repetitions.

Intended Goal Amount of Reps
Muscle Growth 8-12
Strength Training 3-5

Lateral Raises

The lateral raise is one of the most popular lateral deltoid exercises, for good reason. It is a great exercise to develop the shoulder. With its many variations (you can perform it standing up, or seated), it is a great exercise to isolate the lateral deltoid. Make sure you keep a slight bend in the elbow and do not go above shoulder height to minimize shoulder cuff injury risk.

- - -

For this exercise, you want to focus on hypertrophy and working the muscles through volume. We recommend using a relatively light weight and aiming to do 12-15 repetitions.

- - -

Leaning Lateral Raises

The leaning lateral raise is a popular variation of the normal lateral raise. By performing it leaning, you increase the range of motion and are able to activate better mind muscle connection. Follow the same rules as the normal lateral raise; keep a slight bend in the elbows and do not go above shoulder height to minimize shoulder cuff injury risk.

- - -

For this exercise, you want to focus on hypertrophy and working the muscles through volume. We recommend using a relatively light weight and aiming to do 12-15 repetitions.

- - -

Front Raises

The front raise is very similar to the lateral raise in movement, but targets a different head of the deltoid. It targets the front head of the deltoid, which is usually a secondary target of many chest exercises. It is less popular than the lateral raise, but is still get a good exercise. Much like the lateral raise, keep a slight bend in your elbow and make sure to not go above shoulder height to minimize the risk of shoulder cuff injury.

- - -

For this exercise, you want to focus on hypertrophy and working the muscles through volume. We recommend using a relatively light weight and aiming to do 12-15 repetitions.

Rear Delt Row

The rear delt row is a great alternative to the face pulls if you do not have cables accessible to you. You can perform this movement with either a barbell or dumbbell, but we prefer dumbbells. Focus on time under tension to ensure that your rear delts are being trained.

- - -

We recommend that you use a relatively light weight in order to obtain optimal form. It can be easy to cheat the exercise by using momentum, which will decrease the amount of work the rear delt has to do.

- - -

Rear Delt Fly

The rear delt fly is a great exercise that many people do as an alternative to cable rear delt exercises. Use light weight and make sure that you are not sacrificing form and using momentum to bring up the dumbbells.

- - -

The rear delt fly is a difficult movement that some people are unable to perfect. Be sure to review the video to see how to do the exercise correctly. We recommend that you use lighter weight to minimize injury risk.

The single arm lateral raise is a cable exercise that mimics the lateral raise performed by a dumbbell. Using a cable is great if you want to work on time under tension and mine muscle connection, as it forces the lateral deltoid to be activated throughout the movement.

For this exercise, you want to focus on hypertrophy and working the muscles through volume. We recommend that you use moderate weight where you are able to do 12-15 repetitions, with the last few repetitions being particularly difficult. Do not sacrifice form for weight, as you could increase risk for injury.

Face Pulls

The face pull is a great exercise that targets the rear deltoid. It is the most popular rear delt exercise, and a ton of people use it to develop the muscle. Try to keep your wrists away from your head.

- - -

For this exercise, you should aim to do 12-15 repetitions of moderate weight. Challenge yourself and make sure that you are feeling the "squeeze" in your rear delt.

- - -

Cable Front Raise

The cable front raise is a great exercise to hit the front deltoid. This exercise is a great alternative if you do not have dumbbells accessible to you. Using cables is a great way to practice time under tension.

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend that you do 12-15 repetitions with relatively light volume.

Intended Goal Amount of Reps
Muscle Growth 8-12

Cable Shoulder Press

The cable shoulder press is the cable version of the seated/standing dumbbell press. This exercises targets the shoulders and is a great way to get additional intensity on your deltoid muscles.

- - -

For this exercise, you want to use moderately heavy weight, but want to get around 12-15 repetitions in. This seems odd, but use a "heavy" weight where you struggle on your last few reps while not compromising form.

- - -

Lying Face Pulls

The lying face pull is a great variation of the face pull that eliminates any use of momentum in the exercise. It really forces you to hit your rear delt without recruiting any other muscle groups primarily.

- - -

For this exercise, you should aim to do 12-15 repetitions of moderate weight. Challenge yourself and make sure that you are feeling the "squeeze" in your rear delt.
