Barbell Deadlift

The barbell deadlift is one of the most popular compound exercise that engages your hamstrings, glutes, lats, and abs. It's a great exercise to start your lift with.

- - -

Please make sure that you are doing these with proper form. For reference, please watch the video above. It's important that you do not use your back at all during this exercise, and focus on shifting the weight to your hamstrings, glutes, and lats. You do not want lower back pain!

Intended Goal Amount of Reps
Muscle Growth 6-12
Strength Training 3-5

- - -

Bent Over Barbell Row (Wide Overhand Grip)

The bent over barbell row is one of the most popular back exercises for complete back growth. With its many possible grips, there is a ton of variation for targetting specific parts of your back.

- - -

For back exercises, we typically want you to use moderate/heavy weight, while still performing quality reps. If you are isolating a specific part of your back(lats, etc.) you may want to go a little bit lighter and focus on time under tension.

- - -

T-Bar Row

The T-Bar Row is a great exercise that targets your inner back.

- - -

For this exercise, you should use moderate weight and make sure that you are not cheating the reps. Keep your chest on the pad, or your chest still, and feel the contraction with your back muscles.

Intended Goal Amount of Reps
Muscle Growth 6-12
Strength Training 3-5

- - -

Chest Supported Barbell Row

The chest supported barbell row brings a unique twist on the normal bent over barbell row. It eliminates any possible cheating, and really shifts the focus to your back.

- - -

Use moderate weight and focus on the contraction of your muscles, especially the eccentric movement.

- - -

Dumbbell Row

The dumbbell row is a great exercise performed with dumbbells that targets the lat muscles. It is a common at-home lat exercise.

- - -

Use relatively heavy weight and row to your hip. You want to make sure you are rowing back rather than straight up so that you are getting the most emphasis place on the lat rather than the shoulder.

- - -

Dumbbell Bent-Over Row

The dumbbell bent over row is the dumbbell equivalent of the bent over barbell row. It is a great exercise, albeit quite uncommon since a barbell is quite common. We prefer the barbell exercise, but if necessary, the dumbbell alternative is solid.

- - -

Reap the most benefits from this exercise by using moderately heavy weight and minimizing the amount of momentum used for each rep.

- - -

Incline Dumbbell Row

The incline dumbbell row is a great exercise for the lats specifically, but the overall back.

- - -

Use moderate weight, and make sure that you are not cheating and recruiting other muscle groups. Keep the focus on the back.

Intended Goal Amount of Reps
Muscle Growth 6-12
Strength Training 3-5

- - -

Dumbbell Pullover

The dumbbell pullover is a great exercise for those using the Arnold split (Chest/Back together on the same day).

- - -

Use moderate weight and feel the exercise in both your lat muscles and in your chest muscles. Be sure to not use your whole body on the movement back towards the top.

- - -

Renegade Row

The renegade row is a great core stabilizing exercise that simultaneously works out the lat muscles.

- - -

The renegade row is an active, calorie-burning exercise. Use about 2/3rd of the weight you would use for the dumbbell row for this exercise.

- - -

Farmers Carry

The farmers carry is a great exercise that many implement in the cardio regimen. It's a great exercise that simultaneously hits many muscle groups.

- - -

Use heavy weight, and challenge yourself on the walk. Do not swing the weight, and keep the weight as close to your hips as possible.

- - -

Seated Cable Row

The seated cable row is a popular back exercise that targets the entirety of the back. There are two ways to perform this exercise: strict or using a bit of momentum. We prefer strict, but the momentum can be good if you are looking to move more weight.

- - -

Use relatively heavy weight and focus on recruiting the entire back for this exercise. If the weight is heavy at the end, do use a bit of momentum, but still focus on recruiting the back.

- - -

Lateral Pull-down

The lateral pulldown is the quintessential lat exercise. With a multitude of grips, you can be sure that you can target various parts with more emphasis in this position.

- - -

For this, lean back a little bit, and use a grip that is a bit wider than shoulder grip. Use an overhand grip. This is the optimal position for the most lat growth and recruitment (Jeremy Ethier). Go relatively heavy and make sure to focus on those lats!

- - -

One Arm Seated Cable Row

The one arm seated cable row is similar to the seated cable row performed with both hands on one attachment. This exercise, however, targets the lats more.

- - -

Use moderate weight and focus on using the lat to move the cable. Do not twist your body to pull the weight towards you.

- - -

Lat Pushdowns

The lat pushdown is also a great exercise performed with a straight bar that targets the lat muscles.

- - -

Use moderate weight, as it becoming heavy may make it a little tough to pushdown. Try to minimize how much legs you use in this, and focus on pushing your arms to your hips.

Intended Goal Amount of Reps
Muscle Growth 6-12
Strength Training 3-5

- - -

Pull Ups

The pull up is one of the most popular, if not the most popular exercise to exist. It is a great back builder when performed correctly.

- - -

There is a progression to pull ups. If you are unable to perform them, perform negative/assisted pull ups (negative is where you start at the top and slowly lower yourself, assisted uses a machine). Do this until you are able to do clean pull ups. A rule of thumb: The further apart your hands are, the more you will recruit the outside of your back. If you have a narrow grip, you will recruit your inner back more than your lats.

Chinups

The chin up is a similar movement to the pull up. The chin up uses a reverse grip, which puts more emphasis on the bicep muscle during the movement.

- - -

The chin up is great for PPL splits (Back, Biceps, and Legs). It's also an easier exercise to do than the classic pull up.
