Barbell Back Squat

The barbell back squat is the quintessential leg exercise, and is the most popular lower body compound exercise. It recruits all of your lower body, but primarily targets your hamstrings.

- - -

There are various ways to train for the barbell back squat. If you are looking to make your muscles look good ğŸ˜, go for lighter weight and more repetitions. If you're training for strength, increase the weight, and decrease the repetitions.

Romanian Deadlift

The RDL is a great complimentary workout to the back squat, as it also engages the hamstrings and glutes.

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use moderate weight with more repetitions (8-12). Be sure to keep the emphasis on your hamstrings, and brace your core! Do not use your back.

- - -

Barbell Split Squat

The barbell split squat is a great exercise that engages all of our lower body and also targets our core strength.

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use moderate weight with more repetitions (8-12). Be sure to keep the emphasis on your hamstrings, and brace your core! Do not use your back.

- - -

Hip Thrusts

The hip thrust is a great exercise that targets our glute and hip muscles.

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use moderate weight with more repetitions (8-12). Be sure to keep the emphasis on your hamstrings, and brace your core! Do not use your back.

- - -

Barbell Deadlift

The barbell deadlift is one of the most popular compound exercise that engages your hamstrings, glutes, lats, and abs. It's a great exercise to start your lift with.

- - -

Please make sure that you are doing these with proper form. For reference, please watch the video above. It's important that you do not use your back at all during this exercise, and focus on shifting the weight to your hamstrings, glutes, and lats. You do not want lower back pain!

Goblet Squat

The goblet squat is a great exercise that targets the quads. If a barbell is not accessible, it is a great way to mimic the front squat movement.

- - -

Please make sure that you are doing these with proper form. For reference, please watch the video above. For this exercise, you'll want to use moderately heavy weight for around 8-10 repetitions.

- - -

Bulgarian Split Squat

The bulgarian split squat is a notoriously difficult exercise, but one that is great for quad and overall lower body growth.

- - -

You'll want to make sure that when you go down on this movement, you are sinking your body towards the bench and not straight down. Going straight down will put unnecessary weight on your knee ligaments (more prone to injury).

- - -

Lateral Lunge

The lateral lunge is a great exercise that targets the hip area and can help increase your mobility in the pelvic area.

- - -

You'll want to make sure that you are not over-extending and placing unnecessary weight on your knees. Keep your center of mass normal and do not lean too far one way.

- - -

Reverse Lunge

The reverse lunge is a great exercise to target your quads, in a similar movement to the barbell split squat.

- - -

You'll want to make sure that you are not over-extending and placing unnecessary weight on your knees. Do not let your knees go past your toes, and keep your weight on your heels.

- - -

Dumbbell RDL

The dumbbell RDL is a similar movement to the barbell RDL, just with a small switch in the equipment used.

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use moderate weight with more repetitions (8-12). Be sure to keep the emphasis on your hamstrings, and brace your core! Do not use your back.

- - -

Calf Raises

The calf raise is a great exercise to target the calf muscle group, which is often neglected in most lower body workouts.

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use moderate weight with more repetitions (12-15). Focus on the eccentric movement, and make sure that you squeeze at the top of the rep.

- - -

Single Leg RDL

The single leg RDL is a similar movement to the barbell RDL, just with a small switch in the equipment used and with more emphasis on core stability.

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use moderate weight with more repetitions (12-15). Focus on the eccentric movement, and make each rep slow and steady.

- - -

Hack Squat

The hack squat is a great exercise to hit the quads, and the machine is just an amazing way to practice hitting full depth.

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use moderate weight with more repetitions (8-12). Focus on the eccentric movement, and make each rep slow and steady.

- - -

Leg Extension

The leg extension is a great exercise to target the quads in a stationary way. You should really feel the burn with this one!

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use moderate weight with more repetitions (10-12). Focus on the eccentric movement, and make each rep slow and steady. Squeeze at the top of each rep.

- - -

Leg Press

The leg press is a great exercise to test your lower body's strength. It's focus can depend on the positioning of your feet. For now, we advise you to use a neutral stance (shoulder-width apart) and in the middle of the platform.

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use heavy weight with less repetitions (6-8). Make each rep slow and steady, and make sure not to lock your knees at the top of the lift. Squeeze at the top of each rep.

- - -

Hamstring Curl

The hamstring curl is a great exercise to target your hamstrings in a stationary position. Like the leg extension, you should really feel this one!

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use moderate weight with more repetitions (10-12). Make each rep slow and steady, and be sure to keep the tension in the hamstrings at all time (do not let the weight slam down). Squeeze at the top of each rep.

- - -

For this exercise, we recommend you use light weight with more repetitions (12-15). Make each rep slow and steady, and really squeeze at the top of each rep.
